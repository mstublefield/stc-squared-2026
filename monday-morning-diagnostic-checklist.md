# Monday Morning Diagnostic Checklist

**A self-diagnostic tool for any team, any industry.**
Pick one lens. Spend 30 minutes. See what you find.

---

## 1. How Your Team Spends Time Together

Watch how time is actually allocated – not how people think it is.

- [ ] How many hours did your team spend in meetings last week? How many in focused, uninterrupted work?
- [ ] Which meetings produced decisions or moved work forward? Which ones were status updates that could have been an email?
- [ ] Does your team have protected time for deep work, or is the calendar a wall-to-wall grid of commitments?
- [ ] When someone has a complex problem to solve, do they have time and space to actually think about it?

## 2. How Information Actually Flows

The real communication happens in the gaps – what's not being said publicly.

- [ ] When's the last time someone on your team publicly disagreed with a deadline or commitment?
- [ ] Are important decisions being made in meetings where everyone's present, or in side conversations between two or three people?
- [ ] If something is going wrong, how long does it take for leadership to hear about it? Days? Weeks? Never?
- [ ] Is there a gap between what your team says in public channels and what they say in private messages?

## 3. How Work Moves Through Your System

Follow one unit of work from start to finish. Most elapsed time is waiting, not working.

- [ ] Pick a recent deliverable. How long did it take end-to-end? How much of that was actual work versus waiting, handoffs, or rework?
- [ ] Where does work most often get stuck? Waiting for approval? Waiting for requirements? Waiting for a review?
- [ ] How often does work start before the requirements are clear – and then need to be redone?
- [ ] If you traced a single task through your system, how many people would it touch before it was done?

## 4. What the Numbers Actually Say

The data is usually there. It's just not being looked at the right way.

- [ ] How long does a typical unit of work take from start to finish? Is that number what you'd expect?
- [ ] Is your team shipping more or less than they were six months ago – even though headcount may have grown?
- [ ] What percentage of your team's effort goes into rework, bug fixes, or redoing things that weren't right the first time?
- [ ] What metrics are you tracking? Are they measuring what matters, or just what's easy to measure?

## 5. What People Believe vs. What They Say Out Loud

The distance between what people tell leadership and what they actually believe is your biggest blind spot.

- [ ] If your team could tell you one thing anonymously – no consequences – what would it be?
- [ ] When was the last time someone raised a concern that changed a decision? If it's been a while, is it because there are no concerns, or because people don't feel safe raising them?
- [ ] Do people on your team believe the current plan will work? Have you actually asked them?
- [ ] What's the thing everyone on the team knows but nobody says out loud?
